pop_region <- pop_joined %>%
  as.data.frame() %>%  # will remove geometry, change from sf to dataframe
  group_by(region) %>%
  summarise(total_pop = sum(population))

sf & Tidyverse note: The group_by() and summarize() functions can also be used on sf objects to summarize within a dataset and combine geometries. Many of the tidyverse functions have methods specific for sf objects, some of which have additional arguments that wouldn’t be relevant to the data.frame methods. You can run ?sf::tidyverse to get documentation on the tidyverse sf methods.
